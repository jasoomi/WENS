Complete Science & Technology Education

Physics

Physics

Physics is the natural science that studies matter, energy, and the fundamental forces of the universe.

Mechanics

Mechanics studies motion and forces.

  • Kinematics: motion without forces.
  • Dynamics: forces causing motion.
  • Statics: forces in equilibrium.
  • Formulas: F=ma, v=u+at, s=ut+½at²
  • Applications: engineering, robotics, vehicles.

Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics studies heat, energy, and temperature.

  • First Law: ΔU = Q - W
  • Second Law: Entropy always increases
  • Third Law: Absolute zero entropy
  • Applications: engines, refrigerators, climate science

Electromagnetism

Studies electric and magnetic fields.

  • Formulas: F = q(E + v × B)
  • Maxwell's equations describe fields.
  • Applications: motors, generators, electronics

Chemistry

Chemistry

Chemistry studies matter, its composition, structure, properties, and reactions.

Organic Chemistry

Study of carbon-containing compounds.

  • Applications: medicines, plastics, fuels
  • Formulas: CH₄, C₂H₆O, C₆H₁₂O₆

Inorganic Chemistry

Study of non-carbon compounds.

  • Applications: materials, catalysts, pigments

Physical Chemistry

Study of energy and matter interactions.

  • Formulas: PV=nRT, ΔG=ΔH-TΔS
  • Applications: reaction kinetics, thermodynamics

Biology

Biology

Biology studies living organisms, their structure, function, growth, and evolution.

Genetics

Study of genes, heredity, and variation.

  • Formulas: DNA base pairing A=T, C≡G
  • Applications: medicine, agriculture

Cell Biology

Study of cell structure and functions.

  • Applications: biotechnology, disease research

Ecology

Study of organisms and environment interactions.

  • Applications: conservation, environmental management

Mathematics

Mathematics

Study of numbers, quantities, shapes, and patterns.

Algebra

Study of symbols and rules for manipulating them.

  • Formulas: a²+b²=c², ax+b=0
  • Applications: solving equations, modeling

Calculus

Study of change (derivatives) and accumulation (integrals).

  • Formulas: d/dx[f(x)], ∫f(x)dx
  • Applications: physics, engineering, economics

Computer Science

Computer Science

Studies algorithms, computation, and information processing systems.

Algorithms

Step-by-step computational procedures.

  • Applications: software development, AI

Databases

Organizing and managing structured data.

  • Applications: websites, banking, big data

Digital & Technology

Digital Technology

Study of electronic systems, digital devices, and modern computing applications.

Internet of Things (IoT)

Network of interconnected devices exchanging data.

  • Applications: smart homes, wearables, automation

Artificial Intelligence

Simulation of human intelligence in machines.

  • Applications: chatbots, autonomous vehicles, prediction systems
